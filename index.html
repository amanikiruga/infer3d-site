Infer3D: 3D Reconstruction through Inverse Generative Modelling

1Harvard University, 2MIT, 3University of Cambridge

Abstract

We present a method for monocular 3D reconstruction that achieves strong generalization to unseen cameras, a key failure mode for existing feedforward reconstruction networks. State-of-the-art models yield excellent performance on in-distribution images, but result in significant degradation under shifts in camera parameters (e.g., viewpoint) not encountered in training.

We begin by analyzing this limitation and show that feedforward architectures implicitly overfit to the training camera manifold. To address this, we introduce an analysis-by-synthesis framework that augments feedforward inference with an explicit optimization-based inference. Rather than feeding monocular inputs directly into the network, our method optimizes the latent 3D representation to match the input image while remaining close to the network's learned 3D prior.

We demonstrate strong OOD reconstruction accuracy on both controlled synthetic benchmarks and real datasets, while maintaining competitive performance in-distribution. Further, our method uses adaptive test-time computation, spending more compute on difficult OOD examples, and less on in-distribution inputs.

Method

Analysis-by-synthesis inference paradigm. To handle OOD inputs, we employ a test-time optimization loop. We freeze the Image Generator and 2D-to-3D Lifter to preserve the learned prior, and optimize the Latent Code and Rendering Parameters to minimize the reconstruction loss between the rendered reconstruction and the OOD input. This ensures the input to the lifter remains a valid predicted in-distribution image while matching the OOD observation.
